Epidemiological studies
Scholl et al. [16] 2004 n = 1, 082Age: ≤18 y to 32 yDietary assessment: 24-hour recall at 20- and 28-week gestation GI by quintiles Q1: <50 versus Q5: >60 Birth weight SGA/LGA births Dietary GI in the lowest quintile was associated with a statistically significant reduction of 116 g in birth weight, while dietary GI in the highest quintile was associated with a nonsignificant increase in birth weight (50.0 g) after adjustment for potential confounders. Compared to subjects with a dietary GI in Q3, those with a dietary GI in the lowest quintile had a 75% increased risk of giving birth to an SGA infant. No significant association was found between GI (in quintiles) and risk of LGA.
